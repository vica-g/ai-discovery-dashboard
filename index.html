Emerging Patterns (n=10)

  • Companies with large non-engineering workforces = warmest prospects: Flynn (back office), Asurion (non-technical functions), and Rula (ops, finance, legal, marketing, support) are all interested and sharing with teams. The common thread isn't industry — it's having hundreds of non-technical employees who need upskilling but can't self-serve.
  • Tech-forward orgs/leaders = red flag: Suchit (builds agents, will self-serve) and Clarify (AI-native culture, too small). They'll figure it out themselves.
  • Chatbot-level users = sweet spot: Christian, Seth, Aidan, Mansoor, and Jack are all chatbot-only users who want to go further but need structure. The more advanced the person (Suchit = agents, Mackenzie = automating), the less likely they are to buy.
  • "Can't build training in-house" = clear buying trigger: Flynn has nobody internally who can teach AI. Rula has 1 L&D person focused on onboarding. Both are active opportunities. When there's no internal trainer, we're the obvious solution.
  • "Go figure it out with your L&D budget" + performance review pressure = potential entry point: Stepful told employees to self-serve with their stipend but tied promotions to AI effectiveness — creating urgency without structure. Mansoor immediately saw our cohort model as the solution. This pattern likely repeats at orgs without a dedicated AI champion yet.
  • "Skeptic who converts mid-call" = high-value archetype: Mansoor came in philosophically skeptical about AI hype and left wanting to pitch it to his boss. Converts tend to become strong internal champions because their conviction was earned, not assumed.
  • PE/VC operational partners = emerging distribution channel: Nick (Growth Street Partners) instantly saw Leland as a fit for his ~10 portfolio companies, each getting enterprise Claude mandated and hands-on setup days from their internal champion Skylar. The pitch isn't "replace Skylar" — it's "Skylar handles deep integration, Leland handles cohort upskilling." This pattern is distinct from the Essendis (reseller) channel — it's softer, relationship-driven, and routes through GP trust rather than a formal contract. Worth mapping: how many PE/VC operational partners have a Skylar equivalent and could become a channel into 50-100+ seats per firm?

Emerging ICP

  • Org: 250–2,000 employees. Large non-engineering workforce (ops, finance, legal, support, marketing). No dedicated internal AI trainer. Leadership has signaled AI matters but hasn't operationalized it — either no champion yet, or one person just been tapped. Industry-agnostic so far (Healthcare, Financial Services, F&B all green); what matters is workforce composition, not sector.
  • Persona: Head of Ops or equivalent (VP/Director-level). Personally at chatbot stage — using AI but knows they should be doing more. Either already the decision-maker for upskilling (Aidan) or highly motivated to become the internal champion (Seth, Mansoor). Reports to COO or CFO. Has or can unlock an L&D / AI transformation budget separate from standard per-head stipends.
  • Red flags: Tech-forward CEO/leadership (they'll self-serve). Company <50 people (too small, informal knowledge sharing suffices). Fully generic Microsoft/Copilot-only environment with no external vendor appetite.

Methodology Builds

  • Mid-market gap partially filled (n=1): Rula at 600 employees is the first prospect in the 250-999 range — and our strongest. Need more calls in 50-999 range to pressure-test whether mid-market is truly the sweet spot vs. 10,000+ enterprises like Flynn and Asurion.
  • No IC or Manager-level calls yet: All 10 calls are Director+ or Founder. Would be useful to talk to an individual contributor or team manager to understand ground-level AI frustration and whether they'd push upward for structured training.
  • Industry coverage is wide but thin: 1-2 reps per industry for most sectors. Too early to rule any in or out — need 2-3 data points per industry before drawing ICP conclusions by vertical.
  • First 1-9 company + first Real Estate call (Jack, n=1): Solo founders are a personal conversion segment, not B2B. Real estate is a laggard industry — interesting for personal enrollments but enterprise B2B plays will face extreme inertia (institutional investors move like Blackstone).
  • First PE/VC operational partner call (Nick, n=1): Growth Street is not a direct revenue opportunity (tight portco budgets, small deal size) but points to a distribution archetype worth testing. How many PE/growth equity firms with 5-15 portcos have an operational partner doing hands-on AI setup days? That could be a repeatable channel into cohorts of 50-100 seats per firm with low CAC.
